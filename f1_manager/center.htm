Ferrari top down under !

Ferrari were dominant right from the start of the opening session of the 2001 season as Rubens Barrichello set a clear pace to lead Michael Schumacher and the two McLarens home at the end of the first hour of practice. The first free practice session of the 2001 Formula One World Championship kicked off in sunny weather conditions at 11.00am local time Melbourne, Australia.

As temperatures reached 20 degrees centigrade the green light was given signalling the start of the 2001 season. Most the drivers left their garages immediately and performed one shake-down lap of their cars. Newcomer to the F1 grid, Enrique Bernoldi was the first man out in the Arrows followed by Jenson Button, Michael Schumacher and Rubens Barrichello.

After quarter of an hour British American Racing’s new driver, Olivier Panis leads the time-sheets ahead of Arrows’ Jos Verstappen, Nick Heidfeld, Enrique Bernoldi, Luciano Burti and Juan Pablo Monotya. McLaren and Ferrari have yet to set any competitive times. David Coulthard took to the track after 20 minutes of the session had passed and the Scot set the fastest time of the session that far with a 1m31.8s lap. Coulthard’s top time did not stand for long, though, as Michael Schumacher was out on track ready to take the top spot for himself. Schumacher carved his time down to 1m30.7s as Mika Hakkinen set the second fastest time some 0.8s slower than the Ferrari.

Michael Schumacher’s team mate, Rubens Barrichello took to the track after 25 minutes and set the fastest time, only to have Schumacher snatch the top-spot back by setting a lap of 1m30.4s. Half way into the one-hour session Ferrari dominates. Michael Schumacher led Rubens Barrichello, Mika Hakkinen, David Coulthard, Heinz Harald Frentzen and Eddie Irvine. With half the session gone Fisichella, Button and Trulli were yet to set a lap time.

While Jordan mechanics worked on Jarno Trulli’s EJ11 Jenson Button and Giancarlo Fisichella took to the track in their Benetton B201s. Fisichella set the 12th fastest time on his first outing, while Button was left down in 17th. Meanwhile there was a change at the front – McLaren’s Mika Hakkinen bounced back to set the new fastest time of 1m30.081s 0.3s faster than Michael Schumacher.

While the old hands tussled at the front, Minardi’s Fernando Alonso improved his time to climb to 14th in the timing sheets while BMW Williams driver Ralf Schumacher moved up to tenth. Team mate Juan Pablo Montoya improved his time also and lay seventh, 0.4s ahead of the German. Ralf Schumacher meanwhile had a nervous moment as he locked up the wheels of his FW23 and almost hit a tyre wall. Fortunately he recovered to the race track.

With twenty minutes of the session remaining Mika Hakkinen led Michael Schumacher, Rubens Barrichello, David Coulthard, Jos Verstappen and Nick Heidfeld. Heidfeld’s team mate, Kimi Raikkonen lay 14th. Meanwhile Olivier Panis was back on track an looked like he was on a mission as he jumped up to sixth while Michael Schumacher also improved on his time to nudge ahead of Mika Hakkinen by less than 0.1s. Schumacher continued to lap and broke into the 1m20s laps with a fantastic time of 1m29.7s.

With ten minutes of the session remaining Michael Schumacher led Mika Hakkinen by 0.3s. Rubens Barrichello was holding onto third with Coulthard fourth ahead of Frentzen and Panis. Kimi Raikkonen was up to tenth ahead of Juan Pablo Montoya in eleventh. The Benettons were struggling with Button and Fisichella in 15th and 16th respectively. Jarno Trulli meanwhile made it out onto the track and set the seventh fastest time.

With just over five minutes of the session remaining Ferrari’s Rubens Barrichello joined Michael Schumacher in setting a lap time below 1m30s – the Brazilian set the fastest time of the weekend with a 1m29.705s lap. The BMW WilliamsF1 team drivers were on a mission meanwhile as Ralf Schumacher rocketed up to sixth and Montoya up to seventh.
In the final minute of the session Rubens Barrichello extended his lead at the top of the timing sheets by setting a lap in 1m29.056s – some 0.7s faster than team mate Michael Schumacher. Schumacher however was on track and responded setting a new personal best of 1m29.368s. Session Times
