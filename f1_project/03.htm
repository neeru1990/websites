F1 isn't about 22 cars going around in circles; it’s a team sport that involves strategy, timing, and accuracy at the highest level of competition. Global leaders from all leagues of business participate in this sport that demands great investments of time, money and dedication. A sport that runs solely on technology and passion. 17 races were held in the year 2000 which spanned across 5 continents. Every team carried out rigorous testing sessions in the winter before the commencement of the season that included race simulations of every track under different circumstances. But testing alone never decides who the winner will be.
 
Great human virtues are tested and only the most courageous survive. Qualifying position, fuel consumption, tyre-wear, time spent in pit lane, probable weather and ability of the circuit/driver combination to provide overtaking opportunities are just some of the other factors a team has to focus on before the start of every event throughout the year. This project tries to shed some light on what actually goes on in the world of F1. The pain and hard work the engineers and mechanics are exposed to throughout the year in extreme climates, team strategies and problems, the approximate cost, how a team moves from one race venue to another and also how to get a job in this amazing sport are just some of the topics covered in this documentation. It can be rated a success even if one genuine F1 fan is generated.
