Many people wish to get involved in the Formula 1 circus. However, most of the pilots start racing since they were 5 years old and have then majored through the higher formulae. So, if you have still not started racing and can understand this documentation then you are probably too old; besides you need to have a $5-10 million sponsorship backup to be able to get into racing in the first place. However, your options for being a part of the team are still open.

So, as an attempt to help those of you out there who are crazy enough to want to be involved, here are a few suggestions to get you started. It's not going to be easy - that's for certain - so you'd better be very determined and very sure that it's something you really want to do otherwise it's best left alone. Motor sport in general can be a very demanding, uncertain and insecure career! Don't expect to have much time left at home as time schedules in motorsport are very demanding and, in the scarce time you may have at home, all you'll want to do is catch up on your sleep!
 
If your chosen career path is as an engineer, designer, aerodynamicist or electronics boffin, then you're going to need a first-class honours degree in the relevant subject before most of the Formula One teams will consider you. Some motorsport organisations may be prepared to assist you in gaining the relevant academic qualification and, in particular, Reynard Motorsport Group, Prodrive and TWR are very active in encouraging young engineers into a motorsport career.

There will be quite a few Technical Directors and Team Managers of both teams and manufacturers who will be happy to advise you on which courses you should be studying to achieve a job with their company and often a personalised letter to them will bring forth some valuable advice, as well as introducing your name to them for the future. Many of the teams will also have Human Resources departments so contact them and make a friend of the HR manager. She'll know what vacancies are currently available and may keep your name on a priority list if you make a positive impression initially. Worse case, she'll give you good advice on how you can progress and improve your chances of achieving a position within motorsport.

Other available positions in teams may include accountants, IT people, Truck Drivers, Chefs, Marketing & PR people, secretarial and stores people so there are many professions you can choose from and still be involved in motorsport. So, let's assume you've chosen a career path as a mechanic in motor racing. How do you get started? Initially, get a job in your local garage and learn as much as you can about general vehicle mechanics and engineering. Go to night school for a Tech. Course and make sure you attend all the lectures and pass your exams with flying colours. You may need these qualifications later to impress a future employer of your achievement and dedication and don't think you will be the only one applying for a job!

The most obvious next step - and how the majority get involved - is to offer your services to your local Formula Ford or Touring Car team and then work your way up the ladder from there. From there, if you work hard, are truly committed and passionate about what you do, you can progress upwards in the team through mechanicing. Maybe you'll have to initially work for nothing when you just help the team out at weekends but, if you can impress the team sufficiently with your enthusiasm, sooner or later you'll be offered something better and more permanent.
Always remember that the more people you can get to know within motorsport, the greater your chance of securing a permanent position for yourself. Junior team positions tend to get filled very quickly and, because many of the senior personnel in a team have such hectic schedules, it's often whom you know rather than what you know that is important.

When you get to interview, you'll need to persuade the team not only of your work abilities and enthusiasm for the job, but also your ability to act as part of a closely-knit team and your aptitude for working on your own initiative and without constant supervision. Think hard about why the team should choose you over all those other hopefuls sitting alongside you in "Reception" and then don't be afraid to " go for it"! Unconventional and forward-thinking people run most teams and individualism is valued highly. So remember, if you want the job badly enough, there are twelve F1 teams out there you can pick on and chances are one of them will need your help one day! Good Luck!
