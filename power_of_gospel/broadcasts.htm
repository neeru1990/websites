07/22/2007 Restoration at Calvary (Part 3 of 4)

When Adam and Eve sinned against God, it appeared that man forever was separated from God. But praise be to God that He had a plan of restoration that He put in to action at Calvary to restore mankind back to Himself. When sin came into the life of Adam and Eve, they could no longer enjoy the presence of the holy God. They had to hid themselves among the shrubs of the garden. When God sent them away from the garden and set up Cherubim to guard the way to the garden, man was forever separated from God. But Jesus when He died on the cross caused the veil of the temple to be torn into two (Luke 23:45) creating a way to the most holy place symbolizing the restoration of that lost relationship.
07/15/2007 Restoration at Calvary (Part 2 of 4)

When Adam and Eve sinned against God, it appeared that man forever was separated from God. But praise be to God that He had a plan of restoration that He put in to action at Calvary to restore mankind back to Himself. One of things that Adam and Eve lost when they sinned was their dignity. They realized that they were naked and shame covered them. But Jesus, when He died on the cross hung in shame when the Roman soldiers divided His clothes among them (Mark 15:4), taking up the shamefulness of mankind on himselt to restore dignity to each one of us.
07/08/2007 Restoration at Calvary (Part 1 of 4)

God created Adam and Eve and placed them at the Garden of Eden. It was a blessed life where Adam and Eve could enjoy the presence of God all the time; their needs were provided for and they were filled with innocent happiness. All of that came to an end when they sinned against the Lord, disobeying his commandment not to eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. It appeared that man forever was separated from God. But praise be to God that He had a plan of restoration that He put in to action at Calvary to restore mankind back to Himself.
07/01/2007 The Will of God - Thankfulness

Jesus said in Matthew 7:21, "Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father in heaven." I Thessalonians 5:18 says, "In everything give thanks; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you." We ought to give thanks to God in everything, not only in a few things. We ought to quit looking at the glass as half empty and start thanking God for the fullness He has provided us.
06/24/2007 The Will of God - Doing Good

Jesus said in Matthew 7:21, "Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father in heaven." I Peter 2:15, 16 says, "For this is the will of God, that by doing good you may put to silence the ignorance of foolish men, as free, yet not using liberty as a cloak for vice, but as bondservants of God." God calls on us to go good in counter the evil of this world.
06/17/2007 The Will of God - Holiness

Jesus said in Matthew 7:21, "Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father in heaven." I Thessalonians 4:3 says, "For this is the will of God, your sanctification!" To do the will of God means to live in holiness like He who has called us is holy.
06/10/2007 The Will of God - Salvation

Jesus said in Matthew 7:21, "Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father in heaven." In the second message of this sermon series, we meditate on I Timothy 7:21, which says that it is God's will that every one is saved and come to the knowledge of the truth. Are we saved? Salvation is a free gift that Jesus gives to anyone who accepts Him. Listen to the Word and receive Him into your life.
