ISDN PRI T1/E1 and ISDN BRI

North America and International

ISDN (Integrated Services Digital Network) refers to a set of international signaling standards for end-to-end, high-performance digital connectivity. The ISDN standard defines two capacities of digital line: ISDN PRI E-1 lines carry 30 dedicated channels of voice or data traffic (23 dedicated channels plus one manage-ment channel in North America), while ISDN BRI lines offer two channels for lighter-duty purposes.

Any of these ISDN channels may be bundled to form a single logical channel, as with RBS T-1. Yet unlike RBS T-1, two-channel ISDN BRI lines are an affordable digital alternative to ordinary analog lines espe-cially in small office and home office contexts.

On board ISDN BRI interfaces are available for direct connection to ISDN BRI lines (integrated BRI interfaces not available for use in North America. TR114s otherwise connect to ISDN lines via a range of Brooktrout ISDN PRI E-1, ISDN PRI T-1, and ISDN BRI telco line interface cards.
