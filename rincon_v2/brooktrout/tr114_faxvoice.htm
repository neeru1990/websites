Advantages of Intelligent Fax Boards vs. Class 2 Fax Modems
  • Server savings: Class 2 modems, because of the overhead associated with document conversion, can push the limit of PC server processing power with as few as two to four ports. In contrast, intelligent fax boards can support up to 96 ports per server. The upshot: past two to four ports, an additional hidden cost of Class 2 ports can be the purchase of additional PCs.
  • More overseas faxing: Because overseas phone rates are higher, if one sends more than 1 of every 8 faxes overseas, greater savings will result from intelligent fax board use than is shown below.
  • Intangibles: Intelligent fax boards enjoy a higher call completion rate, so less labor is exerted to track down and handle failed call attempts. Productivity increases are greater because more people use intelligent fax board-based fax systems more often. Crises don't occur when workers arrive at the office in the morning to find that 70 faxes out of a 400-site fax broadcast were never actually sent.
Cost Model Assumptions
  • A four port fax server is used to send 160 4-page faxes per day (considered a mid-volume load), 260 days per year.
  • Of the 160 daily faxes, 16 are local and essentially free, 24 are "local" long-distance ($0.15/minute), 104 are long-distance ($0.06/minute), and 16 are international ($0.35/minute).
  • The performance penalties of Class 2 modems (e.g. poor signal-to-noise ratio, prolonged "handshaking," re-sending of failed call attempts) are conservatively assumed to add only 5% to Class 2 calling time.

Source: Brooktrout Technology and Davidson Consulting, Burbank, CA, 2000
Universal PCI (uPCI)

Universal PCI (uPCI) is the ability to plug a Brooktrout intelligent fax board into either a 3.3V or 5V bus slot in a server chassis. A board that is uPCI will be physically ‘keyed’ for both 3.3V and 5V bus slots. That is, there will be a second notch in the PCI connector on the board. The 3.3V notch will be on the left of the connector, closer to the card bracket than the 5V notch (which is on the right).

Brooktrout’s TruFax, TR114 Loop Start, TR114 BRI, TR114 DID, TR114 Combo (Loop/DID), and TR1034 fax cards are all Universal PCI compatible (per PCI-SIG Specification 2.3). All Brooktrout uPCI cards at present are 32-bit, 33MHz, 3.3V/5V universal connector cards. These cards will work in servers that have the following PCI bus slot characteristics up to and including:

  • 64-bit connector
  • 133MHz bus clock speed
  • 3.3V-only bus slots
  • 3.3V-only slot signaling
