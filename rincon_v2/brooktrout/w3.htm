Security Concerns: Can My Fax Boards Be Hacked Into?

The number of LAN Fax server installations has experienced significant growth over the past few years and the potential for growth over the next several years is tremendous. A LAN Fax server can save your company time and money while providing increased security and productivity. Along with your LAN Fax Software, the TR series fax boards (our TR114 line of boards, plus our TruFax boards) provide you with the ability to send and receive faxes from your desktop quickly and efficiently, while at the same time ensuring privacy and security for your faxes. Since the TR series boards are typically installed in servers that are on the network, the threat of someone breaking into your network through the TR114 is an on-going concern that has many IT professionals worried.

Fortunately, for those who are installing the TR series boards, they do not have to worry about the security of their network fax server.

The TR series boards are DSP-based telephony boards that transmit information via the T.30 fax communication protocol. "T.30" is a handshake protocol that describes the overall procedure for establishing and managing communication between two fax devices. The TR series boards do not have any data exchange capability, as do data modems, which is why a hacker could not possibly break into your network through a TR series board. It would be as if someone were trying to break into your network through a fax machine. Having a TR series board in a server that's on your network poses no threat whatsoever to your network security.
