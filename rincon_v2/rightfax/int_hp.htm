Hewlett Packard Integration FAQ

Q. How does RightFax work with the HP 9100C and 8100C Digital Sender?
A. Users can easily scan documents into the RightFax server and manage the documents to send, store, copy and route using the RightFax interface. With the integration of RightFax and the HP 9100C and 8100C Digital Sender, fax numbers can be entered directly on the control panel of the scanner. The document can also be scanned via the HP 9100C and 8100C Digital Sender directly to a network file server, either NetWare® or Windows NT®. The document is then sent to a user’s PC desktop and faxed from there using the RightFax server.

Q. How is a fax sent from the HP 9100C and 8100C?
A. The user simply places the document in the scanner and enters the fax number into the control panel or selects a fax number from a predefined list of destinations. The document is then scanned and sent directly to the RightFax server for transmission. A copy of the fax is stored in the user’s RightFax mailbox.

The user can scan the document and type in the name/number of the mailbox. From there the documents can be distributed electronically within the immediate company or to remote locations such as branch offices or main headquarters via the RightFax network fax server. The scanned document can be combined with other scanned documents or with documents from other application such as Microsoft® Word or Excel.

About the RightFax Product line:

The RightFax line of products provide a total approach to enterprise communications with a complete range of network, production and Internet e-document delivery options:

RightFax network-based solutions provide the desktop and e-mail gateways needed to fax enable any desktop user to send, receive, and manage documents. RightFax network solutions provide a range of server lines and add-on modules to meet the document delivery needs of any size organization.

RightFax production systems, powered by CommercePath™ technology, enable electronic delivery via fax, e-mail or the Internet of business-critical documents such as purchase orders, invoices and statements automatically and unattended from legacy, host or ERP applications. RightFax Internet Services gives access to 6,000 virtual ports to allow for fax delivery via a secure IP connection over the Internet. This outsourced option provides capacity on demand and fax line redundancy and backup in the event of hardware maintenance or downtime.
