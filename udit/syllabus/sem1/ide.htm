B.Sc. IT Syllabus - Year I, Sem. I

Introduction to Digital Electronics

Major Topics

  1. Number Systems and Codes - Binary, Decimal, Octal, Hexadecimal and their inter-conversions.
  2. Codes - BCD, Excess-3, Gray code etc.
  3. Digital electronic signals and switches - Concept on digital signal, logic levels, Active high, Active low signals, Switching Characteristic of Semiconductor diode, Transistor.
  4. Logic Gates - AND, OR, NOT, NOR, NAND, EX-OR, EX-NOR operations and their truth table.
  5. Boolean algebra and reduction techniques - K-Maps and Quine – McClusky.
  6. Arithmetic Operations - Binary Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication, Division. 2’s Complement Subtraction.
  7. Circuits - Half- Adder, Full Adder, Half Subtracter, Full Subtracter, 2-bit by 2-bit Multiplier , Various Code convertors.
  8. Multiplexers (MUX)- Working of MUX, Implementation of expression using MUX.
  9. Demultiplexers (DEMUX) - Implementation of expression using DEMUX, Decoder.
  10. FLIP FLOPs - Concept of Sequential circuit, S-R, J-K , Preset & Clear, Master – Slave J–K D , T Flip Flops their truth tables and excitation tables, Conversion from one type to another type of Flip Flop.
  11. Registers
  12. Logic families and their characteristics - Characteristic of Digital ICs.
Reference
  • Fundamentals Digital electronics by RP Jain, TMG
  • Digital electronics by Derek Molly, PHI
  • Digital Electronics, An Introduction to Theory and Practice William H. Gothmann, Prentice-Hall of India pvt. ltd.
